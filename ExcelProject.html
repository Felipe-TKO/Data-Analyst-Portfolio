Bike Sales Dashboard

I. Overview:

    The objective of the analysis was to identify the factors that contribute to bike sales for a store, including age, gender, region, education, and work commute time.
    The target audience for the analysis was the bike store owner and stakeholders.

II. Approach:

    Excel was used to clean and organize the data, and pivot tables were used to create tables and visuals.
    Line and bar graphs were created to visualize the differences in each type of factor and variable.

III. Results:

    The analysis found that income, age, and gender had significant effects on whether a bike was purchased.
    Specifically, middle-aged people with an income of $50,000 to $60,000 USD were found to be the optimal target audience for marketing.

IV. Conclusion:

    Based on the findings, the report recommends that the bike store owner and stakeholders focus their marketing efforts on middle-aged people with an income in the $50,000 to $60,000 USD range.
    Overall, the analysis was able to identify important factors that contribute to bike sales, which can help the bike store owner and stakeholders make more informed decisions about their marketing and sales strategies.
